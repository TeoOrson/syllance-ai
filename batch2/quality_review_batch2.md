## Summary Observations

**Most reliable dimensions:** Strictness, Formality, Clarity — models generally agree and assessments match researcher judgment

**Least reliable dimensions:** Contestability (systematic underscoring by primary; over-scoring by validation), Affect (primary anchors on harshest language; ignores pedagogical framing), Politeness (primary confuses institutional tone with directive stance)

**Patterns specific to Batch 2 not seen in Batch 1:**
- E-S7 (missing affective tone) is more prevalent — disciplinary mix includes more policies with explicit pedagogical rationale that the primary model ignores
- Validation model over-scores Politeness/Affect/Contestability on blunt prohibition policies (P104, P108) — the opposite of E-S6
- P090 ("No restrictions") represents a new data quality failure mode: policies too minimal to score

**Recommended calibration adjustments before Batch 3:**
1. Add holistic framing instruction to primary scorer: "Consider the overall emotional register and policy posture, not just the most severe phrase"
2. Add contestability calibration examples: "consult with instructor" and "per-assignment authorization" should score ~2.5, not 1.0–1.5
3. Add validation model calibration: blunt prohibition + sanctions language should not score ≥3.5 on Politeness or Affect
4. Flag policies under 50 characters for manual review before scoring — too minimal for reliable automated scoring
